# German Abitur: More good grades, fewer bad grades

The **Abitur** is the German high-school diploma required for university admission. Grades run from **1.0** (best) to **4.0** (worst passing grade) in steps of 0.1. A **1er Abi** — an overall average in the 1.x range — is the highest achievable result. A **2er Abi** sits in the middle, and a **3er Abi** is a passing but below-average result.

The chart shows the share of Abitur graduates in each of the 16 German states (*Bundesländer*) that fell into each grade band, from 2006 to 2024.
