# German Abitur: More good grades, fewer bad grades

The **Abitur** is the German high-school diploma and the gateway to university. Grades run from **1.0** (best) to **4.0** (worst passing grade), in steps of 0.1. A **1er Abi** — finishing with an overall average in the 1.x range — is the highest tier and opens doors to the most competitive university programmes. A **2er Abi** sits in the middle, and a **3er Abi** is a passing but below-average result.

The chart below tracks what share of all Abitur graduates in each German state (*Bundesland*) fell into each of these three bands every year from 2006 to 2024.

![German Abitur grade bands 2006–2024](assets/good_bad_grades.svg)

## What the data shows

Across all 16 states the pattern is striking and consistent:

- **1er Abi (green)** — the share of top graduates has roughly doubled or more since 2006, with a sharp additional spike during the COVID-19 pandemic years (2020–2022) and a partial retreat since.
- **2er Abi (purple)** — the middle band has stayed broadly flat, acting as a stable plateau.
- **3er Abi (orange)** — the share of graduates with below-average results has fallen steadily, compressing toward zero in many states.

The convergence of the green line upward and the orange line downward is visible in every single facet — this is not a quirk of one state but a nationwide structural shift over nearly two decades.
